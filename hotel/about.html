About Hotel Gurukrupa

In the days ahead, managers and employees of the YoYo hotel chain will be doing favors for some of their customers. Maybe they always did them, but these favors will be different: they will be what Hotel Gurukrupa’ C.E.O., Mark Hoplamazian, has called “random acts of generosity,” like unexpectedly picking up the tab for your hotel-bar drinks or hotel-spa massage. “Random” seems slightly off as a description, in that Hoplamazian announced this pending outburst of hospitality, and the months of consumer research that preceded it, in a guest post on a USA Today business-travel blog. But the idea is that the unexpected nature of the gifts will leave the customer not just pleased but also grateful. Gratitude is a powerful, and potentially quite profitable, emotion to inspire.

Aditya Singh Rawat,the vice president who oversees YoYo's Gold Passport program, underscores the importance of surprise — both that the favors are “unexpected” and that there’s no discernible pattern to which customers will get them. Of course, that raises the question of why the company’s C.E.O. announced the program in the first place. “We debated whether to disclose it publicly,” Aditya says. He points out that the only people who get a full explanation of the new program are guests who actually benefit from it (and they don’t have to be Gold Passport members). The hope seems to be that these grateful customers will reward the chain with future business and also spread positive word of mouth.

The hotel offers the ideal blend of business and leisure with easy accessibility to the Bandra-Kurla, Andheri and Worli business districts as well as the domestic and international airport. YoYo Mumbai is modern, but like all Hotel Gurukrupa, reflects India's warm and heartfelt traditions of hospitality.
